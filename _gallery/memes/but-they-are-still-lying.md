But they are still lying

> "We know they are lying, they know they are lying, they know we know they are lying, we know they know we know they are lying, but they are still lying."
> 
> _Aleksandr Isayevich Solzhenitsyn_

This haunting quote masterfully captures the psychology of systemic deception and state propaganda. Though famously attributed to Aleksandr Solzhenitsyn, historians and scholars have actually found no concrete evidence it ever appeared in any of his published works. Its origin remains unverified, but its enduring popularity lies in its hyper-specific breakdown of how authoritarian environments operate.

The quote exposes a societal condition where:

- The lie is an open secret: Both the speaker and the listener know the statement is false.
- Deception is a tool of control: The speaker keeps lying not because they expect to be believed, but to test obedience and force the populace into participating in the charade.
- **Truth is stripped of its power:** It creates a soul-crushing dynamic where objective facts are discarded in favor of ideological performance.
