But they are still lying

> "We know they are lying, they know they are lying, they know we know they are lying, we know they know we know they are lying, but they are still lying."
> 
> _Aleksandr Isayevich Solzhenitsyn_

This haunting quote masterfully captures the psychology of systemic deception and state propaganda. Though famously attributed to Aleksandr Solzhenitsyn, historians and scholars have actually found no concrete evidence it ever appeared in any of his published works. Its origin remains unverified, but its enduring popularity lies in its hyper-specific breakdown of how authoritarian environments operate.

The quote exposes a societal condition where:

- The lie is an open secret: Both the speaker and the listener know the statement is false.
- Deception is a tool of control: The speaker keeps lying not because they expect to be believed, but to test obedience and force the populace into participating in the charade.
- **Truth is stripped of its power:** It creates a soul-crushing dynamic where objective facts are discarded in favor of ideological performance.

**Additional comments:**

The famous words attributed to Aleksandr Solzhenitsyn serve as a stark reminder of the corrosive nature of systemic deceit. This quote maps the complex layers of an open secret where the facade of authority is maintained despite the total collapse of shared reality. It describes a cycle where the liar and the audience are trapped in a performative loop. Everyone involved understands the falsity of the communication, yet the ritual of the lie continues unabated. The deception becomes a mechanism of control rather than a tool for persuasion.

Maintaining a false narrative functions as a test of submission. When authorities broadcast statements that everyone knows are untrue, they demand that the public participate in the fiction. Silence or forced agreement acts as a signal of compliance. This psychological landscape erodes the foundations of objective truth, replacing transparency with a soul crushing game of survival. Understanding these dynamics is essential for identifying how propaganda persists in modern society even when its dishonesty is plain for all to see.
