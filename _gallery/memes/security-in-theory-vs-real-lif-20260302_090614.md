**Additional comments:**

Digital security often occupies a complex space in our minds where we imagine high stakes battles involving massive computing power and intricate algorithms. Tech enthusiasts frequently obsess over the mathematics of 4096-bit RSA encryption, believing that the only way to bypass such defenses is through millions of dollars in hardware and sophisticated code-breaking clusters. This theoretical framework provides a false sense of security that ignores the most vulnerable link in any system. The reality of cybersecurity is far more grounded and much more physical. Adversaries rarely choose to engage in a prolonged digital siege when they can simply exploit human weaknesses. A simple wrench costing five dollars often proves more effective than a server farm when coercion is involved. This classic comic illustrates that robust encryption means very little if the person holding the key can be forced to surrender it. True security requires defending against physical threats just as diligently as digital ones.
