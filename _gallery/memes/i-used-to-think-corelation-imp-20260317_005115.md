I used to think Corelation implied Causation till I took a statistics class

**Additional comments:**

Many people fall into the logical trap of assuming that because two variables move together, one must cause the other. This classic mistake is the foundation of many misconceptions in data analysis and daily life. Taking a statistics class provides the necessary tools to separate meaningful patterns from simple coincidences. It forces a deeper look at confounding variables and the complexities of real world data. While the initial lesson in a statistics course is that correlation does not equal causation, the reality of interpreting data remains nuanced. Even the most educated observers often find themselves questioning their own certainty as they encounter increasingly complex datasets. Learning to think like a statistician is a journey of humility, constant verification, and healthy skepticism regarding how we interpret the world around us.
