To take my hands off of my life and give you control

"Control (Somehow You Want Me)" by Tenth Avenue North is a Christian contemporary song that explores themes of surrender, divine sovereignty, and God's unconditional love.

### **Lyrical Themes**

The song reflects a journey from trying to manage one's own life to finding peace in surrendering control to a higher power.

- **Surrender:** The lyrics "I want to lay them all down " [[00:28](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFfztu8-bBQ&t=28)] and "to take my hands off of my life and give you CONTROL" [[00:59](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFfztu8-bBQ&t=59)] express a desire to release personal anxieties and ambitions.
- **God's Sovereignty:** It emphasizes God's presence through all circumstances, noting He is "behind and before me" [[01:46](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFfztu8-bBQ&t=106)] and that His plans involve redemption and restoration [[01:42](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFfztu8-bBQ&t=102)].
- **Grace and Love:** A central theme is the wonder of God's desire for a relationship with humanity, despite our shortcomings, as heard in the chorus: "God, You don't need me, but somehow You want me" [[01:59](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFfztu8-bBQ&t=119)].

### **Musical Style**

- **Genre:** Christian Pop/Rock.
- **Structure:** It follows a standard verse-chorus-bridge structure, building in intensity from a melodic acoustic opening to a powerful, anthemic chorus.
- **Vocals:** Lead singer Mike Donehey delivers an emotive performance that ranges from intimate during the verses to passionate and high-energy during the climactic sections.
