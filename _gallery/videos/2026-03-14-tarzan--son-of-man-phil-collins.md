## Growing Up in the Jungle: The Upbeat Anthem of "Son of Man"

If "You'll Be in My Heart" is the emotional anchor of Disney's *Tarzan*, then **"Son of Man"** is its undeniable driving heartbeat. Written and performed by the legendary Phil Collins, this track is a masterclass in the classic "coming-of-age montage," propelling the narrative forward with an infectious, uplifting energy.

## The Perfect Montage Soundtrack

In animation, showing a character's growth over years can often feel rushed, but *Tarzan* uses "Son of Man" to make this progression feel earned and triumphant. The song plays as we watch Tarzan transition from a clumsy, awkward human child struggling to keep up with his gorilla peers, into a strong, agile, and fiercely capable adult.

Through trial, error, and sheer determination, we see him learn the ways of the jungle—crafting tools, mastering the vines, and finally earning the respect of his troop. The driving percussion and bright brass instrumentation mirror his relentless perseverance.

## A Message of Growth and Potential

Phil Collins' lyrics are more than just background music; they are a motivational anthem. Lines like *"In learning you will teach, and in teaching you will learn"* perfectly capture the essence of Tarzan's journey. He isn't just surviving in the jungle; he is adapting, innovating, and bridging the gap between his human intellect and his animal upbringing.

The song's core message—that true strength comes from belief, understanding, and patience—resonates deeply. It reminds us that no matter our beginnings, we all have to climb our own mountains and discover our own potential.

## Phil Collins' Enduring Legacy

"Son of Man" is yet another testament to why Phil Collins' work on the *Tarzan* soundtrack is so widely celebrated. He didn't just write pop songs to play over the credits; he wrote the internal monologue of the characters. By blending classic rock sensibilities with jungle-inspired rhythms, he created an energetic, timeless track that still makes you want to get up and conquer the day.
