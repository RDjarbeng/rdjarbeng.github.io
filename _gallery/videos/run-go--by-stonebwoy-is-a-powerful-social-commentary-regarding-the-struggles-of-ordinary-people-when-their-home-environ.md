The Desire for a Simple Life: Throughout the song, particularly around the 30-second mark and again near the 1-minute 30-second mark, Stonebwoy expresses that people simply want a dignified existence. This is defined by basic human needs: food, clothing, love, and shelter.
Critique of Social Injustice: The lyrics address systemic exploitation, such as the commodification of natural resources like oxygen, mentioned around the 20-second and 2-minute 40-second marks. He questions how people are expected to survive when basic necessities are turned into burdens.
Call for Accountability: Moving into the 2nd minute, the song highlights the selfishness of those who prioritize their own wealth at the expense of others, labeling such actions as predatory and unprincipled.

The message concludes by reinforcing that everyone deserves stability and peace, emphasizing that the hardships described are what ultimately force people to consider leaving their motherland.
