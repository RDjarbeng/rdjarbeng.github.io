**Additional comments:**

Prague reveals its artistic soul most vividly after the sun goes down. The Franz Kafka rotating head sculpture stands as a mesmerizing testament to this blend of literature and mechanical innovation. Crafted from forty two rotating stainless steel panels, this towering bust captures the complex psyche of the famous writer through its constant motion and mirrored surfaces. It serves as an essential stop for anyone wandering through the city center at night. Beyond this iconic monument, the historical charm of the city continues with the famous Astronomical Clock. Visitors can witness the intricate medieval clockwork in action while enjoying local treats like the fresh, crisp apples often found at nearby markets. Every corner of Prague offers a new discovery, from its deep historical roots to its modern public art installations. Exploring these sights provides a unique glimpse into the creative heart of the Czech capital.
