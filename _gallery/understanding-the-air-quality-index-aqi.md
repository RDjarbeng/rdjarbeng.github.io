**Additional comments:**

Understanding the Air Quality Index is essential for protecting your health on a daily basis. This standardized system uses a color-coded scale to communicate how clean or polluted the air is in your local area. By tracking ozone and particle pollution levels, the AQI allows you to make informed decisions about outdoor activities and exercise. When levels remain in the green zone, the air is safe for everyone. As the index climbs into the orange, red, purple, and maroon categories, the risks to your respiratory and cardiovascular health increase significantly. Monitoring these fluctuations helps you identify when extra caution is necessary to avoid potential health complications. Stay proactive by checking your local air quality reports regularly. Prioritizing this awareness ensures you can better manage your exposure to harmful pollutants throughout the changing seasons.
