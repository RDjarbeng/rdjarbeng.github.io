# Writing Style Guidelines

When writing or editing posts for this website, follow these rules:

## Punctuation

1. **No em dashes or en dashes**: Do NOT use em dashes (`—`) or en dashes (`–`) anywhere in post content. Do NOT replace them with spaced hyphens (` - `) either, that looks just as unnatural.
   - ❌ `the skill — and the finish`
   - ❌ `the skill - and the finish`
   - ✅ Rewrite the sentence using natural punctuation: commas, periods, semicolons, colons, or parentheses.
   - ✅ `the skill, and the finish`
   - ✅ `The post exploded. Over 52,000 likes...`
   - ✅ `Watch the full sequence: the skill, the drive, and the finish.`

## Tone

1. **Avoid asking questions to the reader** unless the post is explicitly a discussion or Q&A. Posts should make statements, not interview the reader.
   - ❌ `What do you think? Best moment in history?`
   - ❌ `Destiny?`
   - ✅ `Written in the stars.`
   - ✅ `One of the best moments in Premier League history, no doubt.`

2. **Keep the tone matching the post type**:
   - Banter/fun posts: Punchy, declarative statements. No rhetorical questions.
   - Technical posts: Clear, instructive tone.
   - Personal reflection posts: Conversational but still statement-driven.

## Checklist Before Publishing

- [ ] No em dashes (`—`) or en dashes (`–`) in the text
- [ ] No spaced hyphens used as dash substitutes (` - `)
- [ ] No unnecessary questions directed at the reader
- [ ] Tone matches the post type
