That's an incredible shot of the **Artemis II** launch. You've caught a piece of history right there.

**April 6, 2026**, the mission is officially in its fifth day and is currently reaching a major milestone. Having launched from Kennedy Space Center on **April 1**, the Orion spacecraft (named _Integrity_) is just hours away from its closest approach to the Moon.

### Mission Status & Highlights

- **The Big Moment:** Later today, the crew—**Reid Wiseman**, **Victor Glover**, **Christina Koch**, and **Jeremy Hansen**—will perform a lunar flyby, passing about **4,000 miles** above the surface. This "slingshot" maneuver will set them on a free-return trajectory back to Earth.
- **Breaking Records:** This mission marks the first time humans have traveled to the vicinity of the Moon since Apollo 17 in 1972. Victor Glover is the first person of color, Christina Koch the first woman, and Jeremy Hansen the first non-U.S. citizen to make this journey.
- **The Goal:** While they won't be landing on the surface (that's reserved for future missions like Artemis IV), they are currently testing Orion’s life-support systems and manual piloting capabilities in deep space.

The crew just shared some stunning "Hello, World" photos of Earth from their window a few days ago, but the view they're getting of the lunar far side today will be something else entirely. They are scheduled to splash down in the Pacific Ocean on **April 10**.
