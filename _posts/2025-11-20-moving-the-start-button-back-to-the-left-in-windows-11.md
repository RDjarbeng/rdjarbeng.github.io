### How to Move the Start Button to the Left

1.  **Right-click** on any empty spot on the taskbar.
2.  Select **Taskbar settings** from the context menu.
3.  In the settings window, scroll down and click on **Taskbar behaviors**.
4.  Look for the **Taskbar alignment** option and change the dropdown from **Center** to **Left**.

Thatâ€™s it! Your Start button and all pinned icons will instantly snap back to the left side, restoring the classic Windows layout you know and love.

### Why Change It?

For many, this isn't just about aesthetics; it's about efficiency. The bottom-left corner is an "infinite target" (mostly)â€”you can throw your mouse cursor into that corner without looking and click, knowing you'll hit the Start button. With a centered taskbar, the position of the Start button shifts depending on how many icons you have open, requiring you to actually *aim* each time.

By moving it back to the left, you regain that consistent anchor point, potentially speeding up your workflow just a tiny bit every time you launch an app.
