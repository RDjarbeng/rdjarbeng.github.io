Whether you’re a power-user trying to fly through tutorials or just someone tired of aiming for the tiny "play" button, mastering YouTube’s keyboard shortcuts is a total game-changer.

The most important shortcut to remember is **`Shift` + `?**`. This brings up the full, interactive menu of commands directly on your video player so you never have to guess.

---

#### **Playback & Navigation**

These are the buttons you'll use most to control the flow of the video.

* **`k` or `Spacebar**`: Toggle Play/Pause.
* **`j`**: Rewind 10 seconds.
* **`l`**: Fast forward 10 seconds.
* **`Shift` + `p**`: Go to the Previous video in a playlist.
* **`Shift` + `n**`: Go to the Next video.
* **`,` (Comma)**: Move to the previous frame (while paused).
* **`.` (Period)**: Move to the next frame (while paused).
* **`Shift` + `,**`: Decrease playback speed.
* **`Shift` + `.**`: Increase playback speed.
* **`0`–`9**`: Jump to a specific percentage of the video (e.g., `7` takes you to 70%).
* **`Ctrl` + `←` / `→**`: Seek to the previous or next chapter.

---

#### **Screen & Volume Control**

Manage how you're viewing the content without digging through the settings cog.

* **`f`**: Toggle Full Screen.
* **`t`**: Toggle Theater Mode.
* **`i`**: Toggle Miniplayer (keeps the video in the corner while you browse).
* **`m`**: Toggle Mute/Unmute.
* **`Escape`**: Close the miniplayer or any open dialog box.

---

#### **Subtitles & Captions**

Fine-tune your reading experience if you're watching on mute or in a different language.

* **`c`**: Toggle Captions/Subtitles ON/OFF.
* **`o`**: Cycle through text opacity levels.
* **`w`**: Cycle through window opacity levels.
* **`+`**: Increase font size.
* **`-`**: Decrease font size.

---

#### **Spherical (360°) Videos**

If you’re watching immersive content, use these to look around:

* **`w` / `a` / `s` / `d**`: Pan Up, Left, Down, or Right.
* **`]` or `+` (numpad)**: Zoom in.
* **`[` or `-` (numpad)**: Zoom out.

---

> **Pro Tip:** If you're on a laptop without a dedicated numpad, the standard `+` and `-` keys (next to backspace) usually handle the font size and zoom functions.
