**Project Glasswing** is a major cross-industry initiative launched by Anthropic in April 2026 to secure the world's most critical software infrastructure using advanced artificial intelligence.
Here is a breakdown of what the project is, why it was created, and how it works:
## The Catalyst: Claude Mythos Preview
The initiative was sparked by the development of **Claude Mythos Preview**, a new, unreleased general-purpose frontier model from Anthropic.
During testing, Anthropic discovered that Mythos Preview possesses unprecedented cybersecurity capabilities. It can surpass all but the most skilled human hackers at finding and exploiting software vulnerabilities. In just a few weeks, the model autonomously found thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities across every major operating system and web browser, including:
 * A **27-year-old vulnerability in OpenBSD**, an operating system renowned for its security.
 * A **16-year-old flaw in FFmpeg** (a widely used video tool) that traditional automated tests missed 5 million times.
 * Several chained vulnerabilities in the **Linux kernel** that allowed an attacker to escalate to complete machine takeover.
Recognizing that these capabilities will soon proliferate—potentially to malicious actors—Anthropic launched Project Glasswing as an urgent, proactive defense effort to ensure defenders get there first.
## The Alliance
No single company can secure global cyber infrastructure. Project Glasswing brings together a coalition of tech and finance giants to tackle the problem collectively:
 * **The Partners:** Amazon Web Services (AWS), Apple, Broadcom, Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google, JPMorganChase, the Linux Foundation, Microsoft, NVIDIA, and Palo Alto Networks.
 * **The Funding:** Anthropic is committing up to **$100 million in model usage credits** for these partners and over 40 other critical infrastructure organizations. They are also making **$4 million in direct donations** to open-source security organizations like the Alpha-Omega project, OpenSSF, and the Apache Software Foundation.
## How It Works
Participating organizations are granted access to Claude Mythos Preview to act as an AI-powered defender. They are using the model to:
 * Identify and patch vulnerabilities in their own foundational systems and critical open-source codebases.
 * Conduct complex tasks like local vulnerability detection, black box testing, endpoint security, and penetration testing.
Within 90 days of the launch, Anthropic plans to publicly report on the lessons learned and the vulnerabilities fixed. The alliance also aims to publish practical recommendations for modernizing cybersecurity in the AI era, focusing on automated patching, vulnerability disclosure, and secure-by-design practices.
> **The Name:** The project is named after the glasswing butterfly (*Greta oto*). The metaphor represents both the software vulnerabilities themselves (which hide in plain sight like the butterfly's transparent wings) and the transparency the project is advocating for in AI security.
