What if I told you that a tube shaped metal container with elongated metal wings could fly? What would you say? It's like this, right, so we send it moving very fast down a long stretch of road on wheels we made just for this occasion. Then we keep increasing its speed and past a certain break neck speed it starts to lift off the ground and take off from the earth.

## A Crazy Story

 It sounds crazy right? And how do we get this thing to go so fast Well we attach engines to it which propel it forward at an incredible speed with a lot of power. And how do these engines move our metal container so fast? Well it's because there's a big fan that turns with many blades drawing in a lot of air, it draws in air and pushes it out the other end. How does it do this?

### The big fan

 Well there's a chamber within filled with explosive gas that powers the fan? Explosive gas you say, where from that? Oh that comes from fuel we inject into that chamber, after we have compressed that air we sucked in to a high temperature we send in fuel that gets ignited super hot, and this fuel air mixture being so super heated is ejected out our engine by fans with blades we made not to melt at those temperatures, and this sends out a stream of hot air that gives about 40% of the metal containers thrust. Thrust? It's what we call our forward force that moves our metal container forward. What's more that reaction and stream of hot air is what we use to propel our metal container forward as well as spin the fans to draw in more air. This drawing in of air and expelling it out the back from the fans account for the remaining percentage of the metal containers and we call this fan a turbine just to be fancy with the names.

So our explosive gas mixture from fuel, runs our big fan that sucks in air and sends it out the back. And this push from the air leaving our engine creates a reactionary forward force that sends our metal container forward. So that's  how we speed things up.

Because apparently if you push the air in one direction it also pushes back in the opposite direction. Some old guy called Newton wrote about this. Anyway to create more force and balance things out we usually use two of them or multiple pairs  on the left and right side of our metal tube and call it a jet turbine engine just to be fancy with the names. We call  our metal tube a plane.  That's the quick summary. True story.

## The Limits of Crazy

What's more there are even more 'crazy' people who use this hot air to make a type of engine for a type of plane that can go past the speed of sound, you may know them as fighter jets used by the military. Then there's those who say 'we want a bigger explosion' and go way faster than the speed of sound, so they combine a large tank of two separate types of special fuel, turn our metal tube heavenwards, and do a dramatic countdown... 10,9,8... 3,2,1 🔥

It looks a little bit ike this. Some people call it a rocket. Apparently there's a science around this as well.

## The industry

I know it sounds crazy but Now would you believe that this contraption is responsible for something we call the aviation industry and is worth around xx trillion USD and employs so many people across continents? Apparently this jet turbine engine invention is useful for moving goods through the air, also there are people crazy enough to sit inside our metal container as it takes off and lands at another destination. So we use them like buses. We have created glorified bus stations we call airports that most countries have, as well as many big cities where you can catch and depart on a plane.

### People and Jobs

We have entire jobs dedicated to this machine, the driver of it we call a pilot, we also have technicians, we have those people with the glowing sticks who command our gargantuan machine to park and align on the lines we drew on our airport, we even have engineers dedicated to this particular area who call themselves aerospace engineers, I could go on...
