Welcome to my channel today we're making Chinese noodles.

The flavor: Lao Tan Sauerkraut Beef Noodles (117g+toppings)

![Chinese noodles packet on display](/assets/images/personal/chinese-noodles-in-kigali-packet.jpg "Chinese noodles packet on display")

Inside of the packet there are 3 packets. I probably should translate them and read the instructions instead of just sprinkling everything into the boiling water with the noodles but I mean,  how else would you use it?

![Chinese noodles smaller pice packets in the big packet for chinese noodles](/assets/images/personal/chinese-noodles-spice-packets.jpg "Chinese noodles smaller pice packets in the big packet")

## Some pictures with translations

Translation of the packet wrapper in case you need it, courtesy of google lens in google translate. Of course I'm gonna translate some of the items to know what I'm eating. It also helps me know what to expect in terms of spice. Ooh, we have Beef noodles today. I'm not sure what Sauerkraut is but it's probably nothing super strange.

![Translated Chinese noodles packet on display showing Lao Tan Sauerkraut Beef Noodles (117g+toppings)](/assets/images/personal/chinese_noodles_translated_packet.jpg "Translated Chinese noodles packet")

Now I put them all in boiling water and waited for it to cook. Boiled some eggs to add as an accompaniment. Also added a piece of beef I marinated and steamed earlier to spice things up. Let's see how this goes. Fingers crossed.

## Serving:

And we're done. Combined it with some microwaved rice and beef stew. Added eggs, and a piece of actual beef. (not included in the packet 🙂). Most of the vegetables came from the smaller packets that were included in the noodles. It's strange to have that since the regular noodles in Kigali, Indomie, has mostly just spices with no vegetables.

![Serving of chinese noodles in 2 bowls showing noodles and rice and boiled eggs](/assets/images/chinese-noodles-in-kigali-serving.jpg "Serving of chinese noodles in 2 bowls showing noodles and rice and boiled eggs")

Review: 

Tasty, wasn't too soft and didn't break when lifted with a fork. (I don't have chopsticks and if I did I don't know how to use them so don't judge me)

Oh but it wasn't bad, sauce was a bit spicy though, needed to cool my mouth from whatever was making it feel hot, pepper hot, not temperature hot, but it wasn't unbearable compared to most dishes I've eaten so it's a 7/10 for me.
